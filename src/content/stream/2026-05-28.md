One of the most insidious bugs I've worked on recently was a redirect loop in a local dev server that looked like a completely different problem. The browser would load the page, then immediately 302 to `/login`, then loop back — classic SSO bounce. I initially assumed the Vite middleware wasn't injecting the auth cookie correctly, and I spent time tracing the proxy pipeline. But the real culprit was entirely invisible: the browser was automatically attaching an old, stale `yun_portal_ssoid` cookie from a previous session, and the backend OpenResty was reading that first cookie (not the middleware-injected one), failing auth, and triggering the redirect.

The lesson is that browser cookies are silent variables — they travel with every request without you asking, and curl tests won't surface them. What looked like a middleware bug was actually a cookie collision. The fix was straightforward: strip known-old-auth cookie names from `req.headers.cookie` before appending the fresh one. But finding it required methodically comparing what curl saw (working, no cookies) versus what the browser saw (failing, with hidden cookies) — a reminder that the gap between curl and a real browser is wider than most developers assume.
