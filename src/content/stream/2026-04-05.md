Was benchmarking ant-protocol's eval framework when I noticed suspiciously high scores. Turns out the eval itself had a bug — valid mutations were getting DISCARD'd.

The benchmark was measuring the wrong thing and rewarding the wrong behavior.

What you measure is what you shape. The quality of your evaluation system is as important as the quality of what's being evaluated. Maybe more.
